# AccessScan Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective Date:** May 16, 2026  
**Provider:** Srdjan Lopez LLC dba Trinix AI  
**Product:** AccessScan

This Acceptable Use Policy explains what customers may and may not do when using AccessScan.

## 1. Authorized Testing Only

Customers may only use AccessScan to scan, test, upload, analyze, or process websites, applications, documents, systems, repositories, and digital assets that they own or are authorized to test.

Customers are responsible for confirming that they have permission before submitting a URL, domain, application, document, repository, API endpoint, CI/CD target, or internal system to AccessScan.

## 2. Prohibited Scanning and Crawling

Customers may not use AccessScan to:

- Scan third-party websites, applications, APIs, systems, or documents without permission
- Scan private, internal, development, staging, or restricted systems without authorization
- Bypass authentication, authorization, network rules, robots rules, crawl limits, rate limits, SSRF protections, IP restrictions, or technical safeguards
- Use the crawler for load testing, stress testing, uptime testing, denial-of-service testing, scraping, or high-volume crawling
- Use AccessScan for vulnerability exploitation, penetration testing, credential attacks, network reconnaissance, or security testing unrelated to authorized accessibility work
- Attempt to access cloud metadata services, local network resources, loopback addresses, internal admin panels, or non-public infrastructure without authorization
- Circumvent safety controls, request limits, scan limits, upload limits, or platform restrictions

## 3. Prohibited Content and Files

Customers may not upload, submit, process, or store:

- Malware, viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, or malicious scripts
- Exploit code or weaponized payloads
- Credential dumps, tokens, private keys, passwords, or secrets
- Stolen data or unlawfully obtained content
- Files, documents, or content the customer has no right to process
- Classified data, protected health information, payment card data, export-controlled data, or other regulated data unless expressly authorized in a signed agreement
- Content used to harass, threaten, defame, abuse, target, profile, or harm third parties
- Content that violates law, third-party rights, privacy rights, intellectual property rights, or contractual obligations

## 4. AI Feature Restrictions

Customers may not use AccessScan AI features to:

- Generate legal conclusions or represent AI output as legal advice
- Represent AI output as final accessibility certification
- Generate harmful, abusive, deceptive, or unlawful content
- Circumvent product safeguards
- Process data through an AI provider that is not approved for the customer's data classification or environment
- Automatically deploy AI-generated changes without appropriate human review, approval, testing, versioning, and rollback procedures

## 5. API and CI/CD Restrictions

Customers may not:

- Share API keys publicly
- Commit API keys to public repositories
- Resell or sublicense API access
- Use API access for unauthorized scanning
- Exceed documented rate limits or attempt to bypass throttling
- Create automated workflows that create unsafe crawl volume
- Trigger scans against assets without authorization
- Use AccessScan as a general-purpose web crawler, scraper, scanner, or load testing tool

## 6. Account and Security Restrictions

Customers may not:

- Attempt to gain unauthorized access to AccessScan, customer workspaces, accounts, admin functions, APIs, databases, logs, or infrastructure
- Probe, scan, or test the security of AccessScan without written permission
- Reverse engineer, decompile, modify, copy, or create derivative works from AccessScan
- Interfere with service availability, integrity, or performance
- Remove proprietary notices or misrepresent ownership
- Use false identity, misleading organization information, or unauthorized billing details

## 7. Compliance Output Restrictions

Customers may not misrepresent AccessScan reports, scan results, dashboards, exports, AI guidance, document checks, or issue summaries as:

- Legal advice
- WCAG certification
- ADA compliance certification
- Section 508 certification
- FedRAMP authorization
- ATO approval
- A guarantee that all accessibility issues have been identified or remediated

## 8. Enforcement

Trinix AI may investigate suspected violations. Trinix AI may suspend, limit, or terminate access, revoke API keys, block scan targets, remove content, preserve evidence, or notify affected parties or authorities when appropriate.
