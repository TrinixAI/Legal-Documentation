# AccessScan AI Disclaimer

**Effective Date:** May 16, 2026  
**Provider:** Srdjan Lopez LLC dba Trinix AI  
**Product:** AccessScan

AccessScan includes AI-assisted features that may help explain accessibility issues, generate remediation guidance, summarize findings, draft action plans, answer Section 508 and WCAG questions, and support workflow decisions.

## 1. AI Output Is Guidance Only

AI-generated output is informational guidance only. It is not legal advice, accessibility certification, engineering approval, procurement approval, security approval, or a substitute for qualified professional review.

Customers must review, test, and validate all AI-generated content before relying on it.

## 2. No Compliance Certification

AccessScan AI features do not certify compliance with WCAG, ADA, Section 508, EN 301 549, or any other law, regulation, policy, procurement standard, or accessibility requirement.

AI output should not be used as the sole basis for legal, compliance, production, procurement, audit, or public reporting decisions.

## 3. Human Review Required

AI-generated suggestions may be incorrect, incomplete, outdated, inconsistent, or unsuitable for a customer's codebase, design system, CMS, document workflow, security boundary, or policy environment.

Before using AI output, customers should confirm:

- The issue actually exists
- The proposed fix meets the applicable accessibility requirement
- The proposed fix works with assistive technology
- The proposed fix does not break design, content, security, performance, or functionality
- The proposed fix is approved through the customer's normal review and change process

## 4. AI Provider Configurations

AccessScan may support different AI provider configurations, including Trinix AI-managed providers, customer-selected providers, cloud AI providers, Azure OpenAI, OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, or local models.

Data handling may vary by provider and deployment model. Customers are responsible for approving the AI provider for their data classification, security boundary, procurement rules, and internal policies.

## 5. Data Sent to AI Features

When AI features are enabled, AccessScan may send limited relevant context to the configured AI provider or model. This may include:

- User prompts
- Accessibility findings
- WCAG or Section 508 references
- Affected selectors
- HTML snippets
- Code snippets
- Document excerpts
- Issue metadata
- Project context
- Remediation status
- Platform context, such as Drupal, Appian, Salesforce, SharePoint, or Microsoft 365

Customers should not enable AI features for sensitive or regulated data unless their agreement, deployment, provider approval, and security review support that use case.

## 6. No Public Model Training by Default

Trinix AI does not use customer content to train public AI models unless expressly stated in a separate written agreement or customer-enabled setting.

Third-party AI providers may have their own terms, controls, and data handling practices. Customers should review the applicable provider terms before enabling or approving AI processing.

## 7. Automated Actions

AccessScan currently identifies issues and provides guidance. It does not automatically modify source files unless a separately scoped feature, integration, or customer-controlled workflow authorizes that behavior.

Any automated or AI-assisted change workflow should include:

- Role-based permissions
- Human approval
- Version control
- Testing
- Rollback capability
- Audit logging

## 8. Customer Responsibility

Customers remain responsible for accessibility decisions, remediation decisions, production changes, compliance statements, and legal representations.

AI can speed up review and remediation, but it cannot replace qualified accessibility testing, legal review, engineering review, or organizational approval.
