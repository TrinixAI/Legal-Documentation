# Integrity Scanner Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Srdjan Lopez LLC dba Trinix AI ("Trinix AI," "we," "us," or "our") handles information in connection with Integrity Scanner ("Integrity Scanner," "the App," or "the Product").

Integrity Scanner is a local Windows desktop utility for integrity and posture review workflows. The App is designed to create local report packets for operator review. It is not a certification service, anti-cheat enforcement system, antivirus product, malware removal tool, remote monitoring tool, or cloud scanning service.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to Integrity Scanner and related product support for the App.

This Privacy Policy does not apply to data handled by Microsoft, the Microsoft Store, Windows, Discord, game publishers, tournament operators, server administrators, or any third party that receives a report from you outside the App.

## 2. Local-first design

Integrity Scanner is designed around local processing.

The App does not, by default:

- Upload scan reports to Trinix AI.
- Upload telemetry to Trinix AI.
- Run cloud scanning.
- Run background monitoring.
- Remotely enforce any action.
- Disable devices.
- Delete files, drivers, or folders.
- Modify Windows registry values.
- Install kernel drivers.
- Claim that a device is clean.
- Claim that cheating, malware, or tampering is confirmed.

Reports are generated locally on the user's device unless the user chooses to export, copy, email, upload, or otherwise share them.

## 3. Information the App may access or process locally

Depending on the feature used, Integrity Scanner may access or process the following local information to create a report or verify a report bundle:

- Windows device and operating system information.
- Local security posture information, such as Secure Boot, TPM, virtualization, code integrity, or related Windows posture context where available.
- Passive anti-cheat ecosystem context, such as known service or binary presence, status, or path information where available.
- Local file paths, file metadata, file hashes, and declared path inventory information relevant to the selected scan or review workflow.
- Report metadata, including timestamps, package version, policy status, confidence context, warning context, and verifier output.
- Optional challenge or nonce metadata provided by an operator or user for replay-resistant review workflows.
- Public key records, signature sidecars, and trusted key registry information used to sign or verify report bundles.
- User-selected files, folders, reports, signatures, or trusted key files selected through App dialogs.

The App is not intended to collect passwords, private keys, payment card numbers, government identification numbers, health information, or unrelated personal files. Users should not place private keys, secrets, passwords, tokens, or unrelated personal documents into report bundles or support requests.

## 4. Information Trinix AI receives

Trinix AI does not receive local scan reports by default.

Trinix AI may receive information if you voluntarily contact us, send support materials, submit screenshots, attach report files, provide diagnostic notes, or otherwise share information with us. This may include:

- Your name or email address if you contact support.
- Your support request content.
- Files or screenshots you choose to send.
- App version, Windows version, error messages, or troubleshooting details you choose to provide.
- Report files or verification files you choose to share.

## 5. How we use information

We use information only for legitimate product and support purposes, including:

- Providing App functionality.
- Generating local reports and verification outputs.
- Helping users troubleshoot installation, packaging, signing, report export, or verification issues.
- Reviewing support requests.
- Improving documentation, safety wording, and user workflows.
- Responding to legal, security, or compliance obligations when required.

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information.

## 6. Report sharing and operator review

Integrity Scanner reports are meant for local or private operator review.

If you share a report with an administrator, tournament operator, league, community, employer, support contact, or other third party, that recipient controls how they handle the copy you shared with them. You should review that recipient's policies before sharing a report.

A verified report signature means the report and signature match cryptographically. It does not prove that a user is clean. It does not prove that all cheating methods, tampering methods, malware, or policy issues are absent.

## 7. Local storage and retention

Integrity Scanner may create local files such as reports, signatures, trusted key files, logs, generated packets, or support artifacts.

Local files remain on the user's device or chosen storage location until the user deletes them, moves them, exports them, or shares them. The App may not control copies made outside the App.

Users are responsible for protecting their local report folders, exported packets, trusted key registries, and any files they choose to share. Users should avoid storing report bundles in public or shared folders unless that is intended.

## 8. Support data retention

When you send information to Trinix AI for support, we retain it only for as long as reasonably needed to respond to the request, maintain business records, protect the Product, or comply with legal obligations.

You may request deletion of support materials you provided, subject to legal, security, abuse-prevention, or operational retention needs.

## 9. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect support information we receive.

No system can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Local report files are controlled by the user's device, Windows account, storage location, and security configuration. Users should use appropriate device protection, access controls, backups, and encryption options such as BitLocker where appropriate.

## 10. Private keys and signing material

Users and operators should never send private signing keys, PFX files, full key folders, passwords, tokens, or secrets to Trinix AI unless we explicitly provide a secure and approved process for that specific purpose.

For normal report verification workflows, only public key material, report files, and signature sidecars should be shared.

## 11. Third-party services

Integrity Scanner may be distributed through the Microsoft Store. Microsoft may process information related to Store access, downloads, crash reporting, purchases, account use, device management, or Store policy compliance under Microsoft's own terms and privacy statements.

If a user shares a report through email, cloud storage, chat, ticket systems, Discord, or other services, those services may process the shared content under their own policies.

## 12. Children's privacy

Integrity Scanner is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through product support. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, contact us so we can review and delete it where appropriate.

## 13. Your choices and rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, or a copy of personal information you provided to us.

You can also delete local reports and App-generated files from your own device at any time, subject to your device permissions and backups.
